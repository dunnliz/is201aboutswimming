Fundamental Strokes & Techniques

Understanding the four competitive strokes is the foundation of effective swimming. Each requires a unique blend of power and hydrodynamics.

Overview of the Four Strokes

Here are the four main swimming strokes used in competitive swimming, each with its own techniques and purposes:

  1. Freestyle

    The fastest stroke, characterized by continuous arm rotation and flutter kick. Key focus areas include body rotation and high-elbow pull.

    • High-Elbow Catch (Early vertical forearm)
    • With a Symmetrical Body Rotation
    • Consistent 6 or 8-Beat Kick Timing Depending on Your Size

  2. Backstroke

    A stroke performed on the back, offering excellent lung capacity practice. Look for consistent hand entry outside the shoulder line.

  3. Breaststroke

    The slowest, but most complex, stroke. It involves a pull and kick synchronization often referred to as a 'glide' phase. This stroke is all about the timing between the pull and kick. If you are slightly off you will be too fast, and if you move your arms to fast you will spin water.

  4. Butterfly

    A physically demanding stroke requiring a powerful, dolphin kick and simultaneous arm pull. Focus on rhythm for efficiency. Strong lats required.
